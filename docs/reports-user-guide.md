# OMB Accounting - Reports User Guide

## Overview

The Reports module provides comprehensive financial reporting capabilities, including Trial Balance, Balance Sheet, Profit & Loss, General Ledger, and Cash Flow statements. This guide will help you understand how to use each report and make the most of the available features.

---

## Accessing Reports

### Navigation

1. **Dashboard**: Click on the "Reports" tab in the main navigation menu
2. **Direct URL**: Navigate to `/reports`

### Report Types

The Reports module includes **5 different report types**:

1. **Trial Balance** - Shows all account balances at a specific date
2. **Balance Sheet** - Shows assets, liabilities, and equity at a specific date
3. **Profit & Loss** - Shows revenue, expenses, and net income for a period
4. **General Ledger** - Shows all transactions for all accounts
5. **Cash Flow Statement** - Shows cash inflows and outflows by activity type

---

## Trial Balance Report

### What is a Trial Balance?

A Trial Balance is a summary of all account balances at a specific date. It helps verify that total debits equal total credits.

### How to Use

1. **Default View**: The Trial Balance shows all accounts with their current balances
2. **As-Of Date Picker**: Use the date picker to view balances at any specific date
3. **Export**: Click "Export PDF" to download the report

### Understanding the Columns

- **Account Code**: Unique identifier for the account
- **Account Name**: Description of the account
- **Debit**: Total debit amount
- **Credit**: Total credit amount
- **Balance**: Net balance (Debit - Credit)

### Common Uses

- **Account Reconciliation**: Verify account balances are correct
- **Financial Snapshot**: Get a quick overview of account positions
- **Audit Preparation**: Prepare trial balance for financial statement preparation

## Balance Sheet Report

### What is a Balance Sheet?

A Balance Sheet shows the company's financial position at a specific date, including assets, liabilities, and equity.

### How to Use

1. **Default View**: Shows all assets, liabilities, and equity
2. **As-Of Date Picker**: Use the date picker to view the balance sheet at any date
3. **Export**: Click "Export PDF" to download the report

### Understanding the Sections

#### Assets

- **Current Assets**: Cash, accounts receivable, inventory
- **Fixed Assets**: Property, equipment, vehicles
- **Other Assets**: Long-term investments, intangible assets

#### Liabilities

- **Current Liabilities**: Accounts payable, short-term debt
- **Long-term Liabilities**: Long-term debt, loans
- **Other Liabilities**: Accrued expenses, provisions

#### Equity

- **Owner's Equity**: Owner's capital, retained earnings
- **Other Equity**: Additional paid-in capital

### Common Uses

- **Financial Position**: Understand what the company owns vs. owes
- **Liquidity Analysis**: Assess short-term and long-term financial health
- **Credit Applications**: Provide financial statements to lenders

---

## Profit & Loss (P&L) Report

### What is a Profit & Loss Statement?

A Profit & Loss (P&L) statement shows revenue, expenses, and net income over a specific period.

### How to Use

1. **Date Range Picker**: Select a start and end date for the period
2. **Auto-Filter**: The report automatically filters transactions within the selected date range
3. **Export**: Click "Export PDF" to download the report

### Understanding the Sections

#### Revenue

- Sales revenue
- Service revenue
- Other income

#### Expenses

- Cost of goods sold
- Operating expenses
- Non-operating expenses

#### Net Income

- Revenue - Expenses = Net Income
- Positive = Profit
- Negative = Loss

### Common Uses

- **Profitability Analysis**: Understand how much profit the business made
- **Expense Tracking**: Identify major expense categories
- **Performance Evaluation**: Compare P&L across periods

---

## General Ledger Report

### What is a General Ledger?

The General Ledger shows all transactions for all accounts, providing a detailed view of financial activity.

### How to Use

1. **Date Range Picker**: Select a start and end date to filter transactions
2. **Auto-Filter**: The report automatically filters transactions within the selected date range
3. **Export**: Click "Export PDF" to download the report

### Understanding the Columns

- **Date**: Transaction date
- **Entry Number**: Unique identifier for each transaction
- **Description**: Description of the transaction
- **Debit**: Debit amount
- **Credit**: Credit amount
- **Balance**: Running balance

### Common Uses

- **Transaction Audit**: Review all transactions for a period
- **Account Reconciliation**: Verify transaction accuracy
- **Financial Analysis**: Deep dive into account-specific transactions

---

## Cash Flow Statement Report

### What is a Cash Flow Statement?

A Cash Flow Statement shows cash inflows and outflows classified by activity type (operating, investing, financing).

### How to Use

1. **Date Range Picker**: Select a start and end date for the period
2. **Auto-Filter**: The report automatically filters cash flow activities within the selected date range
3. **Export**: Click "Export PDF" to download the report

### Understanding the Sections

#### Operating Activities (經營活動)

- Net Income
- Changes in working capital (receivables, payables, inventory)
- Depreciation and amortization

#### Investing Activities (投資活動)

- Purchase of fixed assets
- Sale of fixed assets
- Investment purchases/sales

#### Financing Activities (融資活動)

- Debt proceeds/repayments
- Owner/shareholder distributions
- Equity transactions

#### Net Change in Cash

- Beginning Cash Balance
- Net Change in Cash (Operating + Investing + Financing)
- Ending Cash Balance

### Common Uses

- **Cash Management**: Understand how cash is generated and used
- **Liquidity Planning**: Plan for future cash needs
- **Financial Health**: Assess the company's ability to generate cash

## Date Range Filtering

### General Ledger & P&L & Cash Flow

These reports support **date range filtering**:

1. **Default Range**: Last 12 months by default
2. **Custom Range**: Select any start and end date
3. **Real-time**: Changes take effect immediately

### Trial Balance & Balance Sheet

These reports support **as-of date filtering**:

1. **Default Date**: Current date
2. **Custom Date**: Select any date to view balances at that point in time
3. **Real-time**: Changes take effect immediately

## Export Functionality

### PDF Export

All reports support PDF export:

1. **Click "Export PDF" button**
2. **Download**: Report is automatically downloaded as a PDF file
3. **Filename**: Auto-generated with report type and date range

### PDF Features

- **Professional Formatting**: Clean, readable layout
- **Color Coding**: Different sections have different colors
- **Complete Data**: All data from the selected date range
- **Print-Ready**: Optimized for printing and sharing

## Troubleshooting

### Reports Not Updating

**Problem**: Changes don't appear immediately

**Solution**:

1. Wait a few seconds for the report to regenerate
2. Click the "Refresh" button if available
3. Clear browser cache if needed

### Export Not Working

**Problem**: PDF export fails

**Solution**:

1. Check internet connection
2. Ensure browser permissions are allowed
3. Try a different browser

### Date Range Not Working

**Problem**: Date filter doesn't apply

**Solution**:

1. Verify date range is valid (start date ≤ end date)
2. Check that the date range includes transactions
3. Try selecting a different date range

## FAQs

**Q: Can I customize the Chart of Accounts?**
A: Yes, you can add, edit, and delete accounts in the Settings module.

**Q: Can I compare different periods?**
A: Yes, use the Comparison feature in the P&L and Balance Sheet reports to compare periods.

**Q: Can I print reports directly?**
A: Yes, PDF export works well for printing and sharing.

**Q: How often should I generate reports?**
A: Monthly for routine operations, quarterly for analysis, annually for tax and compliance.

**Q: Can I export to other formats?**
A: Currently, PDF export is supported. Additional formats may be added in future updates.
